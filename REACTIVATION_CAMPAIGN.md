## Part 1 — Strategic Diagnosis

### What likely went wrong

1. **The product was abandoned** — no welcome sequence, no activation emails, no follow-up. Users signed up into a void and left because nothing nudged them back.
2. **The website had a broken or confusing flow** — users hit an error or unclear UX when trying to unlock contacts. The friction of a broken experience is permanent. They will not retry by default.
3. **No value demonstration at the right moment** — even if users searched and found contacts, nothing pushed them to unlock one, and nothing explained what they would get if they did.
4. **Free unlocks were probably unexplained or unseen** — if users didn't know they had 3 free unlocks, they left without ever experiencing the core product.
5. **No social proof, no liveness signals** — a product that feels abandoned or half-built will not attract payment.
6. **No urgency and no follow-up** — people who sign up and do not hear anything within 48 hours forget the product exists.

### What the reactivation campaign needs to achieve

In priority order:

1. **Rebuild trust** — some users had a bad experience. Acknowledge it without dwelling on it.
2. **Re-establish relevance** — remind users why they signed up. Their need is probably still alive.
3. **Get one action** — a search, a login, a free unlock. One small action reactivates intent.
4. **Identify high-intent users** — who replies, who clicks, who searches, who unlocks, who upgrades.
5. **Convert 5–20 users to paid** — Pro at £39/month or Agency at £149/month.

### User psychology

These users have three distinct mindsets:

**Group A — The Forgotten (≈ 60%)**  
They signed up, got busy, forgot. They still have the need that caused them to sign up. They are not hostile. A clear, honest email that reminds them of the product will be enough to get them to click.

**Group B — The Burned (≈ 25%)**  
They signed up, hit a broken flow, and left with a mildly negative impression. They need to be disarmed quickly. The worst thing you can do is send them a normal marketing email. They need acknowledgement that something was broken, and evidence that it is now fixed.

**Group C — The Casual (≈ 15%)**  
They signed up out of curiosity, have no immediate need, and will not convert on this campaign. Do not optimise for them. Let them unsubscribe and focus on Groups A and B.

### Key objections

| Objection | How to handle it |
|---|---|
| "Is this thing even still working?" | Show the database is live. Use real numbers: 55,016 contacts, 42,614 with email. |
| "Is the data any good?" | Name specific categories. Scouts, academy directors, agents, club staff. Give examples. |
| "It was broken before." | Acknowledge it directly in Email 1. Don't be defensive. Short and clear. |
| "£39 is a lot for something I haven't used." | Get them to use it first. Free unlocks before any payment ask. |
| "I can find these people on LinkedIn for free." | LinkedIn has 900 million people. Footy Contacts has 55,000 football-specific people. It is the filter they can't build themselves. |
| "I don't know what I'd do with it." | Give them a job to do. "Search for a scout at a Championship club" or "find the head of academy at any League One side." |

### The strongest conversion angle

**Specificity + time-saving**.

The story is not "big database" — it is "the only place that has already done the work of finding every scout, agent, academy director, and club contact in professional football and putting them in one searchable place."

The person who pays £39/month is not buying a database. They are buying the 20–40 hours of research they won't have to do themselves.

Frame every email through this lens.

## Part 10 — Pre-Send Product Audit

**This is a hard gate. Do not send any emails until every step below passes.**

0 unlocks ever recorded from 743 registered users is a strong signal that something in the product flow was broken. Before sending warm users back to a broken experience, verify the entire conversion path yourself — in a fresh browser session, using a real account.

### Test these 10 things in order

1. **Login from email link**: Can a user sign in via magic link or standard login from a fresh browser?
2. **Landing after login**: Does `/app` load immediately after login — not a dashboard, not a profile page?
3. **Search loads**: Does a search query return results without errors?
4. **Results are meaningful**: Are results relevant to the search term? Do names, roles, and organisations show?
5. **Lock state is clear**: Are locked fields obviously gated? Is it clear what an unlock gives you?
6. **Free unlock works**: Can a user unlock a contact without entering a card?
7. **Unlock reveals details**: Does the unlock correctly show email, phone, and LinkedIn?
8. **Counter updates**: Does the free unlock counter go from 3 → 2 after one unlock?
9. **Pro checkout works**: Does the upgrade flow reach Stripe checkout without errors?
10. **Account updates after payment**: After a test Stripe payment, does the account reflect Pro status?

If any of these fail, fix it before sending. One broken step in this chain wastes your warmest users and makes the campaign worse than sending nothing.

## Part 12 — Winback Offer Decision

### Options evaluated

| Offer | Pros | Cons | Verdict |
|---|---|---|---|
| **50% off first month (£19 → £39)** | Low friction. One-time. Feels like a fair deal, not desperation. Easy to explain. | Does not lock in long-term value. Some users will churn after month 1. | ✅ **Recommended** |
| **£19/month for first 3 months** | More compelling on paper. Extends the trial period. | Complex to implement. Harder to explain. Trains users to expect discounts. | ❌ Avoid |
| **Free trial (7 or 14 days)** | Very low friction. Easy to say yes. | You're at 1 paying subscriber — you need revenue, not trials. Trials attract non-buyers. | ❌ Avoid |
| **Extra unlocks (e.g. 10 extra free)** | Keeps users on free. Gets product usage. | Does not generate revenue. Only useful if you have no paying users at all. | ⚠️ Use only in Email 3, not as conversion offer |
| **Annual discount (33% off at £390/year)** | Better LTV. Committed buyer. | Harder sell to a first-time user. Barrier is too high for reactivation. | ⚠️ Mention, don't lead with |
| **No discount** | Protects price integrity. | Slower conversion for a product with no proven reputation. | ❌ Not right for this moment |

### Recommendation: 50% off first month

**Why this works for your situation:**

1. You need revenue now. A paying subscriber at £19 is infinitely better than a non-subscriber at £0.
2. It does not cheapen the product — it is framed as a "comeback" offer for people who never got a fair shot at it.
3. It is time-limited (7 days) which creates genuine urgency.
4. It is easy to implement in Stripe as a one-time coupon code or automatic discount.
5. After month 1, the subscriber is on £39/month. If they found value, they stay. If they don't, you learned something.

**Implementation**: Create a Stripe coupon for 50% off first month. Apply it automatically via checkout URL parameter. Or create a unique code: `COMEBACK50`. Include a hard end date in the email.

## Part 13 — Product-Led Activation Flow

### What should users land on after clicking?

**CTA**: `footycontacts.com/app` — the search interface, not the homepage.

The homepage explains the product. Users already know what it is — they signed up. Send them directly to the search screen. Reduce clicks to first action.

If users are not logged in, they should see:
1. Login screen (prefilled email if possible via magic link)
2. After login → search interface immediately

Do not redirect to a dashboard or profile page. The first screen after login should be a search field.

### What should happen after they search?

- Show results immediately, blurred/locked where applicable
- Show a clear "You have 3 free unlocks this month" banner in the search results
- Show contact category tags (scout, agent, academy, etc.) so users can see the type of person
- Show the name and club/organisation — enough to confirm the person exists
- Hide email, phone, LinkedIn behind the unlock

This is the key tension that drives conversion: enough information to confirm the person is real, not enough to use without unlocking.

### What should happen after they use 1 free unlock?

Trigger an in-app message or email:

> "You've used 1 of your 3 free unlocks this month. 2 remaining. [Upgrade to Pro for 150 unlocks/month →]"

This is the moment where intent is highest. Do not wait. Send the upgrade message immediately after the first unlock event.

### What should happen after all 3 free unlocks are used?

- Show a hard gate on any further unlock attempts
- Message: "You've used all 3 free unlocks for this month. Pro gives you 150 unlocks from £39/month."
- CTA: Upgrade to Pro
- Do not hide the search results — let users keep searching, keep seeing who's there. Just gate the unlock action.

Showing users what they cannot have is more powerful than hiding the product entirely.

### What should happen if they don't upgrade after free unlocks run out?

- Day 1 after exhaustion: email — "You used all your free unlocks. Here's what you found."
- Day 3 after exhaustion: email — "Your unlocks reset on [date]. Or upgrade now to keep searching."
- Day 7 after exhaustion: email — "Comeback offer expires [date]." (if applicable)

### What should happen if they never upgrade?

They stay on free. The free plan is a permanent conversion surface. Every month when unlocks reset, there is a re-engagement moment. Build a monthly trigger email: "Your 3 free unlocks just reset."

## Part 15 — Compliance and Deliverability

### Are you allowed to email these users?

These are existing registered users, not a cold list. That gives you a stronger basis than cold outreach — but these emails are partly marketing (especially the discount and Pro push), not purely service communications. Do not treat the whole sequence as transactional.

**The relevant legal frameworks in the UK are GDPR and PECR.** Under PECR, the soft opt-in rule may apply if users gave their contact details in the context of a service like Footy Contacts and were given a clear opportunity to opt out at signup. If that opt-out opportunity was absent at signup, you should rely on consent or legitimate interests — but note that individuals have an absolute right to object to direct marketing under GDPR, which must be honoured immediately.

**Before sending, confirm:**
- Users who previously unsubscribed or are on the suppression list are excluded
- You are only emailing about Footy Contacts or closely related services
- Every email identifies you and Footy Contacts clearly
- Every email explains why the user is receiving it
- Every email has a working one-click unsubscribe
- You stop emailing any user who objects or unsubscribes, immediately

**The required footer for every email:**
> *You're receiving this because you created a Footy Contacts account at footycontacts.com. [Unsubscribe] if you don't want to hear from us.*

**If in doubt**: The ICO's direct marketing guidance covers GDPR and PECR in detail. For a list of 743 users, the practical risk is manageable if you include a clear unsubscribe and honour it — but the legal basis matters more as you scale.

### How to send safely to 743 users

**Do not blast all 743 in one go on Day 0.** Warm up deliverability:

| Batch | Size | Time |
|---|---|---|
| Batch 1 | 150 | Tuesday 9am |
| Batch 2 | 250 | Tuesday 11am |
| Batch 3 | 343 | Tuesday 2pm |

This spreads the load over a few hours. At 743 total, you are well within Resend's free/starter limits. Staggering also means if something breaks (wrong link, typo), you can pause after Batch 1.

**Before sending:**
- Confirm SPF, DKIM, DMARC are configured for your sending domain (Resend handles this, but verify)
- Send a test to yourself and 2–3 real email addresses first
- Check the email does not trigger obvious spam signals (no ALL CAPS in subject, no excessive links, no image-only emails)
- Verify your unsubscribe link actually works

**Handling unsubscribes:**
- Resend tracks unsubscribes automatically if you use their built-in unsubscribe header
- Alternatively, use a suppression list in your email tool and check before each send
- Never re-add an unsubscribed user

**Suppression list:**
You have a `20260428_dedup_suppressions.sql` migration — confirm your suppression logic is active and working before sending. Do not email any suppressed addresses.

## Part 17 — Reply Handling

Replies are your highest-value leads. Treat every reply like a sales call.

**Reply received → respond within 4 hours (ideally less)**

### Reply templates

**When someone replies with a contact type (e.g. "scouts"):**

> Hi [Name], thanks for replying. Scouts are well covered in the database — we have chief scouts, regional scouts, and international scouts across Premier League, Championship, League One/Two, and multiple European leagues. You have [X] free unlocks on your account — go search here: [link]. Let me know if you can't find the specific person and I'll check manually.

**When someone replies saying the product was broken before:**

> I appreciate you saying that. You're right — there were real issues earlier in the year. I've gone back through the core flows and fixed them. If you hit anything broken now, please reply and I'll fix it same day. Worth trying your free unlocks — no card needed.

**When someone asks about pricing:**

> Happy to explain. Free plan gives you 3 unlocks a month — good for testing. Pro at £39/month gives you 150 unlocks and 75 exports — good if you're actively making outreach. Agency at £149/month is unlimited unlocks, 500 exports — good for agencies, clubs, and scouting departments. What's your use case? I can tell you which one makes sense.

**When someone says they're interested in Agency:**

> Great to hear. Agency gives you unlimited unlocks and 500 exports a month. Most users on that plan are agents managing multiple clients, scouting ops, or agencies doing volume outreach. Happy to walk you through it — or you can go straight to [upgrade link]. Also happy to chat if you want to talk through whether it fits what you're doing.

## Summary: The One-Page Version

**Problem**: 743 users, 0 conversions, 1 paying subscriber. Product was not given the attention it needed after launch, and had real UX issues.

**Approach**: Honest founder-led relaunch. Acknowledge the problem. Show the database is real and alive. Get users to take one small action (search, unlock). Convert the highest-intent 5–20 into paid.

**The 5-email sequence**:
1. Honest relaunch (Day 0)
2. What can you search? (Day 3)
3. Free unlock nudge (Day 6)
4. Reply generation — "what are you looking for?" (Day 10)
5. Pro conversion — £19 first month (Day 14)

**Plus**: Behavioural triggers (see Part 3) fire based on what users actually do in the product. These are more effective than any broadcast email and should be built after the sequence runs.

**The one offer**: 50% off first month (£19 instead of £39). Time-limited, clearly explained, no code needed.

**The one action per email**: one link, one CTA, one job for the reader. No menu of options.

**Revenue goal**: 5–20 paying subscribers = £195–£780/month MRR minimum.

**The test of success**: If you get 5 paying subscribers from this campaign, the unit economics of organic acquisition are proven and you can start investing in growth.
